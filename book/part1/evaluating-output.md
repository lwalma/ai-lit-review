# Summary Part 1: Effective and Responsible GenAI Use

For effective and responsible GenAI use, keep the following principles in mind:

1.	**Prompt**: Effective GenAI use starts with an effective prompt. Make your prompt specific, using (relevant elements from) the format: clear task-description + persona + context + format + tone + exemplars. Use follow-up prompts to further optimise the output.
2.	**Proof**: Always check GenAI output for inaccuracies and biases. Critically evaluate the quality of the output, by using your own critical thinking skills, by using your own knowledge about the topic and academic practices and by comparing the output with 'traditional' sources.
3.	**Privacy**: There are many unknowns about how GenAI tools use your input. Never feed personal details or confidential information to GenAI tools and only insert work from others if you have their explicit consent.
4.	**Planet**: The evironmental impact of GenAI is significant. Take this into account when considering using GenAI tools.
5.	**Presentation**: GenAI tools may generate grammatically correct sentences, but the output is often generic and flavourless. Don’t forget about your own voice and creativity when you are writing a text.
6.	**Property**: In the end, always remember that you are responsible for your work, also when using GenAI. Do not oursource your research and writing tasks to a GenAI tool, but use it as an assistent to complement your own skills.
