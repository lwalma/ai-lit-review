# Reporting about Your Use of AI

When you use AI tools in the literature research or writing process, it is important to be transparant about how you used AI. Therefore you have to report about your AI use in your text. 

## Why Reporting about AI Use Is Important

For students it is important to report about your AI use so that your supervisor or lecturer can see if you used AI responsibly. They also want to see if your AI use follows the AI guidelines of your university or faculty (if it has any) and that you comply to academic standards (i.e., use AI responsibly). Also for   want to know how their students used AI, but academic journals and publishers are also placing increasing importance on transparency regarding AI use.

Also for academics who want to publish their work, it is important to be transparant about AI use. Scientific journals and publishers are placing increasing importance about reporting about AI use in order to uphold academic integrity and high quality publications. These journals and publisher all emphasise that the author(s) always remain responsible for any errors that are due to ineffective AI use.

## How to Report about AI Use

There is not just one correct way to report about AI use. For students, it can depend on your university, faculty, study program or even course how your supervisor or lecturer wants you to report about your use of AI. Scientific journals and publishers also have their own specific way in which they want authors to disclose their AI use. 

```{warning}
Always consult the guidelines to make sure you report about your use of AI in the correct way. In the sections below we will discuss examples, but you should **always** check how you have to report about AI use in your specific case.
```

### How to Format an AI Disclosure Statement

There are a lot of different formats that are used for AI disclosure statement. Most common elements that should be included are:
- What tool you used
- What purpose you used the tool for

In addition, you might be asked to also include:
- When you used the tool
- The prompt you used
- How you used the output

For example, <a href="https://www.elsevier.com/about/policies-and-standards/generative-ai-policies-for-journals" target="_blank">Elsevier</a> uses the following template for the AI disclosure statement that authors who publish with Elsevier should include:

During the preparation of this work, the author(s) used [NAME OF TOOL / SERVICE] in order to [REASON]. After using this tool/service, the author(s) reviewed and edited the content as needed and take(s) full responsibility for the content of the published article.

See for other examples the guidelines from:
- <a href="https://www.apa.org/pubs/journals/resources/publishing-tips/policy-generative-ai" target="_blank">APA</a>
- <a href="https://journals.ieeeauthorcenter.ieee.org/become-an-ieee-journal-author/publishing-ethics/guidelines-and-policies/submission-and-peer-review-policies/#ai-generated-content" target="_blank">IEEE</a>
- <a href="https://link.springer.com/brands/springer/journal-policies#Artificial%20intelligence%20(AI)" target="_blank">Springer</a>
- <a href="https://info.arxiv.org/help/moderation/index.html" target="_blank">arXiv</a>

### Where to Include Your AI Disclore Statement

There is no consensus where the AI disclore statement should be included. It could be done:
- In a seperate statement at the end of the text
- In the acknowledgements
- As a general footnote
- As a specific footnote in the sections for which AI was used
- In the method section

It depends on the guidelines from the journal, publisher or university where the AI disclore statement should be added. It can also depend on what AI was used for. For example, if you used an academic AI tool to search sources, this should be mentioned in the Method section of your paper. If you used it to generate an image, this can best be reported in the caption of the image. If you used AI for grammar and spelling check only, this can be done in a seperate statement or in the acknowledgements.
